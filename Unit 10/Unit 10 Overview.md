## Unit 10: Wrap-up & Graphs III

This unit we will be covering the fundamentals of*graphs*and common ways they appear in interviews.

#### Lecture Session

In lecture, you will focus on applying graph traversal techniques in more complex and interview-style scenarios.

In this lecture, you will...

- Solve graph problems in timed, interview-style settings
- Communicate graph-based solutions clearly and effectively

#### Breakout Session & HackerRank

In the problem sets, you will...

- Become familiar with key terms related to graphs
- Become comfortable identifying and working with various graph representations
- Use Breadth First Search (BFS) and Depth First Search Algorithms (DFS) to traverse graphs

### Session 1: Mock Interview | Graphs

In this session, students will learn about how to build and manipulate different representations of graphs, including lists of edges, adjacency lists, and adjacency matrices. Students will also be introduced to the two primary traversal algorithms for graphs: Breadth First Search and Depth First Search.

### Session 2: Wrap-up | Graphs

In this session, students will continue to explore using Breadth First Search (BFS) and Depth First Search (DFS) algorithms to solve common graph problems. They will learn to manipulate the base algorithm and explore the different use cases of these two traversal algorithms.
